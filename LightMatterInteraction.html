Talk Title: 'High performance room temperature phosphorescence prompted by Hydrogen-bonded organic frameworks'

üåà Have you ever wondered why certain materials glow when light is shone on them? Why do we see colors in objects?? Light is everywhere. But what happens when it meets matter? By the end of this talk, we will explore the nanoscale interactions between light and materials using the fascinating world of spectroscopy.

Studies on room temperature phosphorescence (RTP) cover a rapidly increasing range of topics, including afterglow materials, anti-counterfeiting, bioimaging, sensing, and light-emitting devices, due to its attractive long lifetime, large Stokes shift, and the involvement of triplet states. In the past, inorganic and organometallic compounds were usually used to achieve long-life luminescence; however, such materials possessed intrinsic disadvantages of biotoxicity and undesirable processibility, and instead, metal-free organic molecules with desirable variety and good biocompatibility and processibility are emerging as potential alternatives. In previous studies, the afterglow performance of organic molecules was usually investigated at low temperatures due to unfavorable nonradiative transition and the presence of oxygen quenchers. Therefore, enhancing the RTP performance of organic molecules has garnered considerable attention.
